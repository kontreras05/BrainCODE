# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Students with concentration difficulties struggling with digital distraction. Spanish-first. They use BrainCODE at a desk during study or work sessions — alone, in a quiet room or a library, trying to hold themselves accountable without external supervision. They are self-aware enough to know they get distracted; they need a tool that tracks that honestly but doesn't make them feel bad about it.

## Product Purpose

BrainCODE monitors focus in real time using webcam facial tracking and window activity detection. A brain mascot visualizes cognitive state through expression, color, and animation — acting as a companion that reflects the user's own attention back at them. Sessions are saved as "Jars" that accumulate over time, forming a visible record of consistency.

Success looks like: a student opens the Jars view at the end of the week and feels the pull of their own history — either pride in a full week that creates pressure to maintain it, or the quiet sting of a sparse week that makes them want to fill it.

## Brand Personality

Acompañante, Enfocado, Sereno — a supportive companion that takes focus seriously without being tense about it. The tone is warm and present, like a study partner who's quietly rooting for you. Never clinical, never gamified, never noisy.

## Anti-references

- **Cold/clinical medical UIs** — no sterile white grids, no hospital-chart data density, no diagnostic panel aesthetics.
- **Ultra minimalist** — don't strip warmth or personality in the name of cleanliness. The mascot, color states, and motion are intentional; removing them to look "clean" defeats the product.
- **Information-saturated dashboards** — surfaces should reveal information progressively. Not everything at once.
- **Emoji in UI copy** — the mascot is the personality vehicle. Text labels carry no emoji. Ever.
- **Modern Tech / DevTools UI** — not VSCode, not terminal-native, not developer tooling chrome. No syntax highlighting aesthetics, no monochrome icon grids, no "productivity app for engineers" density.
- **Aurora UI** — no holographic gradients, no glowing mesh backgrounds, no iridescent color washes. The existing OKLCH palette is warm and purposeful; don't dilute it with trend aesthetics.

## Design Principles

1. **Streak weight** — the Jars view should feel like evidence you'd feel bad losing. Past consistency creates forward pull; the UI should make that weight visible and personal, not just statistical.

2. **Companion over judge** — the brain mascot supports, it never scolds. State changes (working → distracted) are observed and reflected, not punished. Copy is always on the user's side.

3. **Calm with substance** — the UI should feel like a quiet room where important things happen. Not empty minimalism, not cluttered maximalism. Information appears when the moment calls for it.

4. **State carries meaning** — color, mascot expression, and motion are not decorative. Each focus state (working, away, social, absent) has a specific visual register. Visual language communicates before text does.

5. **Ceremony for effort** — starting and ending a session deserves acknowledgment. The farewell animation exists because finishing something is worth marking. Micro-moments of recognition compound into motivation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline. `prefers-reduced-motion` is already respected throughout (farewell animation disabled, state transitions simplified). No additional constraints specified — maintain sufficient color contrast across all focus states and UI text, particularly within the dark sidebar.
